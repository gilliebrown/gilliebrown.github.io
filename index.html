I found my first bacteriophage when I was in high school. A few years later, I defended my undergraduate honors thesis over a novel bacteriophage with the ability to infect stationary host cells. While doing phage work, I also enjoyed learning about different electron microscopy techniques. I later spent time working in the Virology Department of a state veterinary diagnostic lab, where I tested for prion diseases in deer and elk. Outside of the lab, I love learning American Sign Language and collecting stickers! I started the Microbiology PhD program at Texas A&M in the fall of 2023 and joined the Ramsey Lab, associated with the Center for Phage Technology.
